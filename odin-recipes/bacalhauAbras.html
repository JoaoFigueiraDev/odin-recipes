Bacalhau Abrás

Bacalhau à Brás is a classic Portuguese dish of shredded salt cod, shoestring potatoes, onions, and creamy scrambled eggs, garnished with parsley and olives.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Ingredients

  • 400/600 g desalted salt cod (bacalhau)
  • 200/500 g shoestring potatoes or thinly sliced potatoes
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2/4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 4/8 large eggs
  • 3/7 tbsp olive oil
  • Black olives, for garnish
  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Preparation

  1. Desalting the Cod: Rinse the salt cod under cold water and soak it in the refrigerator for 24–48 hours, changing the water 2–3 times a day. Once desalted, remove skin and bones, then shred into small flakes by hand.
  2. Preparing the Potatoes: Cut potatoes into thin matchsticks. Rinse to remove excess starch, pat dry, and fry in hot oil until golden and crisp. Alternatively, bake or air-fry for a lighter version.
  3. Cooking the Cod and Aromatics: Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Sauté onions and bay leaves until soft and translucent, about 5–8 minutes. Add garlic and cook briefly until fragrant. Stir in shredded cod and cook for 3–5 minutes until it absorbs the flavors.
  4. Combining with Eggs and Potatoes: Lower the heat. Gradually add lightly beaten eggs, stirring constantly to achieve a creamy texture. Add the fried potatoes and mix gently. Cook until eggs are softly set but still moist.
  5. Finishing Touches: Cover the frying pan and let it cook over low heat for about 15 to 20 minutes, until the meat is tender and the sauce has thickened. If necessary, adjust the salt and pepper
  6. Serve: Season with black pepper and salt if needed. Garnish with chopped parsley and black olives. Serve immediately while warm.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Serving Sugestions

Bacalhau à Brás is typically served as a main course for lunch or dinner. Pair it with a light green salad or a slice of crusty bread, and a crisp Portuguese white wine like Vinho Verde enhances the experience.
